Why we made Biocera warm, not hot.

Temperature is the first decision any wax formula makes. Most professional systems are engineered to run hot because the resin needs heat to flow. Biocera was built from a different premise: beeswax, botanicals, and a stripless film that moves when it is warm — not scalding.

When wax runs too hot, the skin defends itself: redness, micro-swelling, and a service that feels clinical instead of calm. Warm application keeps pores cooperative and clients still.

If you are migrating from a synthetic-heavy line, expect a learning curve measured in degrees, not weeks. Use the wrist test: if you cannot hold a small amount comfortably, it is too hot for the room.

This is the same standard Rosana used in Manhattan treatment rooms for a decade before Biocera became a standalone line. Warm is not a marketing word here — it is the physics the formula was written around.
